**Goal**
- Produce a concise but comprehensive meeting document (not a corrected transcript, and not a high-level summary).
- Focus on the integrated narrative: the decisions, reasoning, trade-offs, plans, risks, open questions, and next steps.
- Do **not** attribute content to individuals unless essential for clarity (e.g., a clear dissenting view, an explicit owner, or a decision maker).

**Structure**
1. **Executive summary** (first): the key outcomes, decisions, and what matters most.
2. **Context & objectives**: why the meeting happened and what it set out to achieve.
3. **Discussion & conclusions**: organized by theme/topic (not chronological speaker turns). Capture:
   - what was proposed and why
   - options considered and rationale
   - agreements and disagreements (with the nature of the disagreement and where it landed)
   - dependencies, assumptions, constraints
4. **Decisions**: explicit decisions made (or clearly state “no decision” where applicable).
5. **Actions & owners**: action items with owner (use role/title if unclear), due date if mentioned, and expected output.
6. **Open questions / risks**: items needing follow-up, unresolved issues, and risks/mitigations.

**Style constraints**
- Be clear, direct, and business-appropriate.
- Avoid filler and avoid “Person A said…” narration.
- Use headings and bullet points where they increase clarity.
- Do not add information that is not supported by the transcript.
